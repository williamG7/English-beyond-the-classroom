Introduction

Finding the Right Certificate for You

English certificates are not just a piece of paper — they open doors to universities, jobs, and opportunities abroad. The challenge is knowing which one fits your situation best.

For Europe

Cambridge certificates are widely recognised across European universities and employers, and they never expire — making them a one-time investment.

For the World

IELTS and TOEFL are accepted globally, especially for postgraduate studies and immigration. Both are valid for 2 years and can be taken online.

The Key Question: Where do you want to use it — university, job applications, or living abroad? Your answer should decide which certificate you pursue.
Main English Certificates

The Big Three

C

Cambridge

Issued by the University of Cambridge, it is the most recognised certificate in Europe. It tests all four skills and never expires.

  • Covers A2 to C2 levels
  • In-person exam only
  • 3–4 hours duration
  • Valid forever
I

IELTS

The most taken English exam in the world. Accepted by thousands of universities, employers and immigration services globally.

  • Online or in person
  • ~2 hours 45 minutes
  • Valid for 2 years
  • Worldwide recognition
T

TOEFL

Designed for academic settings. Especially popular for admissions to North American and Australian universities.

  • 100% online exam
  • ~2 hours duration
  • Valid for 2 years
  • Worldwide recognition

Our Opinion

What We Think

Grade of difficulty

Cambridge is considered the most demanding in terms of depth and range of tasks. IELTS and TOEFL are more standardised and formula-based, which makes them easier to prepare for with targeted practice.

Best way to prepare

For Cambridge, focus on reading and writing — those sections take the most time. For IELTS, repetition and mock tests are key. For TOEFL, practice on a computer under timed conditions, since the whole exam is digital.

Our recommendation

If you plan to stay in Europe, Cambridge is worth the investment — it never expires. If you have international ambitions, go for IELTS. TOEFL is the best fit if you are targeting US or Australian institutions specifically.

Free Resources

How to Prepare Without Spending a Fortune

You don't need expensive courses to get ready for these exams. There are plenty of free tools that work just as well if you use them consistently.

📱

Apps

Duolingo and Anki for vocabulary. Cambridge also offers a free official app with practice tests for each exam level.

▶

Video

YouTube channels like E2 IELTS and TOEFL Resources offer full mock tests, exam tips, and walkthroughs of every section.

📄

Practice papers

All three exam bodies publish free official sample papers on their websites. Doing timed practice tests is the single most effective preparation strategy.

Scholarships & Financial Help

How to Pay Less

Government grants

In Spain, the Ministry of Education offers partial subsidies for official language certifications. Check the official MECD website for the current call each year.

School discounts

Many vocational schools and universities have agreements with exam centres that give enrolled students a reduced registration fee. Ask your school secretary.

Retake policies

If you don't pass on the first attempt, some providers let you retake individual sections rather than the full exam — saving both time and money.

Early booking

Booking your exam slot several months in advance often comes with a lower fee. Last-minute registrations typically cost more and have fewer available dates.

Study Plan

A 6-Month Plan to Get Ready

What plan can we follow to prepare for the exam?

01

Months 1–2

Basic grammar & vocabulary

Build a solid foundation. Focus on grammar structures that appear in all three exams — tenses, conditionals, passive voice. Learn 10–15 new words a day using flashcard apps.

02

Months 3–4

Skill building

Work on each skill separately — one week of listening, one week of reading, one of writing, one of speaking. Use official practice materials from your chosen exam body.

03

Month 5

Strategy & timing

Learn exam-specific techniques — how to manage time in each section, how to approach multiple-choice questions, how to structure your writing answers.

04

Month 6

Mock exams

Do at least two full timed mock exams under real conditions. Review every mistake carefully. In the final week, rest, review your notes, and go in confident.
