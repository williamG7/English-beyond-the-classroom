Part 1

Practice Reading

Practicing reading comprehension with AI can be very effective because AI can adapt texts, ask questions, and give feedback instantly. Here are some practical ways to do it — from generating passages to quizzing yourself interactively.

Recommended Apps

AI Tools for Reading

These are the best AI-powered tools to build your reading comprehension — no textbook needed.

ChatGPT

Generate reading passages at your level, get comprehension questions, and receive detailed explanations for each answer pointing to the exact part of the text.

Gemini

Ask for texts on any topic and adjust difficulty on the fly. Great for vocabulary-in-context exercises and fill-in-the-blank activities.

Deepseek

Excellent at adapting texts to different CEFR levels, generating inference questions, and giving thorough feedback on your summaries.

How to Practice

Key Reading Strategies

Don't just read — read with purpose. These six techniques will improve your comprehension faster than passive reading ever could.

Read → Answer → Check

Read a passage, answer comprehension questions, then ask AI to explain every right and wrong answer with references to the text.

Summarising

After reading, write a short summary in your own words. Ask AI for feedback on what you included, what you missed, and how to improve it.

Vocabulary in Context

Ask AI to pull the 5 most useful words from the passage, explain their meaning, and give you example sentences to use them yourself.

Inference Practice

Answer questions about what the text implies, not just what it says. This is one of the most powerful ways to develop deeper understanding.

Interactive Quizzing

Ask AI to quiz you one question at a time and wait for your answer before continuing. This turns comprehension into an active conversation.

Difficulty Adjustment

Too easy? Ask AI to rewrite the text at C1 level. Too hard? Ask for a B1 version. You are always in control of the challenge.

Daily Routine

10-Minute Daily Reading Plan

Spend just 10 minutes a day and you will notice the difference within weeks. Each step targets a different reading skill.

Time Activity How to do it
3–5 min Read Ask AI for a passage at your level on any topic you enjoy.
3 min Answer questions AI asks 3–5 comprehension questions. Answer without re-reading.
2 min Check explanations Ask AI to correct your answers and explain why each one is right or wrong.
2 min Write a summary Write 2–3 sentences about the text. Ask AI for feedback on what you missed.
Long-Term Progress

1-Week Progression Plan

Mix different text types and skills across the week to build well-rounded reading ability.

Day Text Type Focus
Day 1 News article Main idea + key details (who, what, when, where)
Day 2 Topic of interest (AI-generated) Vocabulary in context — find 5 new words
Day 3 Any passage Comprehension questions — answer and check with AI
Day 4 Short story or opinion text Inference — what does the text imply?
Day 5 Longer passage (300–400 words) Summarising — write 3–4 sentences, get AI feedback
Day 6 Technical or academic text Vocabulary deep dive — synonyms and fill-in-the-blank
Day 7 Re-read a passage from this week Mixed review — redo questions and compare with previous answers
Ready-to-Use

AI Prompts for Reading

Copy and paste any of these prompts into Claude, ChatGPT or any AI chat to start practising right away.

1

"Give me a 200-word reading passage about [topic] for an intermediate English learner. Then ask me 5 comprehension questions about it."

2

"Here are my answers. Tell me which are correct and explain why, pointing to the part of the text that supports each answer."

3

"List 5 useful words from the text, explain their meaning and give me an example sentence for each."

4

"Here is my summary. Improve it and tell me what important information I missed."

5

"Quiz me on this passage. Ask one question at a time and wait for my answer before continuing."

6

"Rewrite this text at B1 level." or "Make the questions harder and add some inference questions."

Part 2

Practice Writing

Writing its one of the most tedious part of practise your English because you usually have to write long texts but with the next prompts you can learn in a very useful way and whatever you want. We made an example of useful prompts and a daily study method.

Recommended Apps

AI Tools for Listening

These are the best AI-powered apps to train your ear anytime, anywhere — no classroom needed.

ChatGPT

Generate writing prompts, get detailed feedback on structure and style, and practice any type of text, from formal emails to creative stories, at any level.

Gemini

Generate writing topics, receive feedback on coherence and vocabulary, and practice drafting texts with a conversational AI that adapts to your needs.

Grammarly

Real-time grammar, punctuation, and style corrections with clear explanations. A great companion for building good writing habits in English.

How to Practice

Key Listening Strategies

Don't just write, write with intention. These six techniques will sharpen your skills faster than unstructured practice ever could.

Free Writing

Set a timer and write without stopping. Don't edit as you go — just let the ideas flow and build fluency.

Imitation Writing

Pick a text you admire and rewrite it in your own words. Study the structure, then mirror it with a different topic.

Prompted Practice

Use an AI to generate a specific prompt — a scenario, a title, a first line. Constraints spark creativity and push you further.

Feedback Loop

Write a text, submit it to an AI, and rewrite it based on the corrections. The revision is where the real learning happens.

Vocabulary Expansion

Ask the AI to highlight weak or repeated words in your text and suggest stronger alternatives. Build range, not just correctness.

Register Awareness

Write the same idea twice — once formally, once informally. Learning to shift register is one of the most practical writing skills you can develop.

Long-Term Progress

1-Week Progression Plan

This plan is focused on the basic needs of a developer who currently works in an office being a SAP developer.

Day Topic Focus
Day 1 What you did at your internship Past tense + clarity
Day 2 Explain something simple (like an ERP system, APIs, frontend/backend…) Clear explanations
Day 3 Write a professional email Formal tone
Day 4 Describe a technical issue and how you solved it Structure + connectors
Day 5 Your typical day at the internship Present simple + frequency
Day 6 Is SAP difficult to learn? Expressing opinions professionally
Day 7 Write freely (work or personal) or rewrite a corrected text Mixed practice + review
Ready-to-Use

AI Prompts for Writing

Copy and paste any of these prompts into Claude, ChatGPT or any AI chat to start practising right away.

1

"Now you are gonna be my English teacher assigning me a writing activity (not to long). After I send you the result you have to correct my text, making emphasis in my mistakes and if you change a little the text and put me a note."

2

"Can you modify the text to make it sound like a native speaker, please?"

3

"What is the meaning of '*something in the text that the AI corrected*'"

4

"I would like to practise my writing skills every day. What would be the best way to improve myself my writing skills? Can you make me a study plan?"

5

"This is great, but I'd like to write about something related to my studies and internship someday. I'm studying cross-platform application development and I'm currently interning at an SAP consulting firm where I have to write several emails and work with ERP systems."

5

"Can you give some sentence to start a text? That part it's always difficult for me. Please (And following all the chat the AI give you the sentences related to the themes)"

Part 3

Practice Listening

Listening is one of the most important skills when learning English. With AI you can practise anytime, anywhere — no teacher needed. AI can speak to you, tell stories, have conversations and even test your understanding. You control the speed, the topic and the difficulty.

Recommended Apps

AI Tools for Listening

These are the best AI-powered apps to train your ear anytime, anywhere — no classroom needed.

Claude / ChatGPT

Generate stories, conversations and dictation exercises on any topic, at any level.

Speak App

Practise real spoken English with an AI conversation partner that gives instant feedback.

ELSA Speak

AI-powered feedback on pronunciation and listening comprehension, tailored to your accent.

How to Practice

Key Listening Strategies

Don't just hear — actively listen. These six techniques will sharpen your ear faster than passive listening ever could.

Active Listening

Focus on every word. Take notes while you listen. Don't just hear — understand.

Shadowing

Repeat what you hear immediately out loud. Great for pronunciation too.

Dictation

Write exactly what the AI says. Then compare and find your mistakes.

Comprehensible Input

Listen to content just one level above yours — not too easy, not too hard.

Accent Exposure

Listen to American, British and Australian English. Real life has many accents.

Predictive Listening

Guess what comes next before the AI continues. Keeps your brain active.

Daily Routine

20-Minute Daily Listening Plan

Spend just 20 minutes a day and you will notice the difference within weeks. Each activity targets a different listening skill.

Time Activity How to do it
5 min AI Story Ask AI for a short story. Listen, then answer 3 comprehension questions.
5 min Dictation AI reads a paragraph. You write what you hear.
5 min Voice Conversation Talk with AI — focus on understanding, not just answering.
5 min Shadowing AI reads sentences one by one. You repeat out loud immediately after.
Long-Term Progress

8-Week Progression Plan

Gradually increase the difficulty as your ear adapts. Start slow — finish at native speed.

Week Focus Level
Weeks 1–2 Everyday vocabulary Slow, neutral accent
Weeks 3–4 Casual conversations Normal speed
Weeks 5–6 Different accents American vs British
Weeks 7–8 Real content Native speed — YouTube, podcasts
Ready-to-Use

AI Prompts for Listening

Copy and paste any of these prompts into Claude, ChatGPT or any AI chat to start practising right away.

1

"Tell me a short story in simple English. Speak slowly. After finishing, ask me 3 comprehension questions."

2

"Read one sentence at a time for shadowing practice. Wait for me to say 'next' before you continue."

3

"Read a short paragraph for dictation. Pause between each sentence."

4

"Let's have a simple conversation in English about daily life. Use basic vocabulary and correct me if I make mistakes."

5

"Tell me the same story three times: once slowly, once at normal speed, once fast."
